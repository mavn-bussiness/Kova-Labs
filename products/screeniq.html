The Origin Story

Built from a real
hospital problem.

"During my ICT internship at a government hospital, patients had no idea which queue they were in, which window to go to, or where to find the pharmacy. Staff printed paper notices and taped them to walls. I knew software could fix this."
— Kova Labs Founder, Hospital ICT Intern 2024

ScreenIQ was born from this experience. It started as a queue display system and evolved into a full digital signage platform — capable of running in hospitals, banks, corporate lobbies, retail stores, and government offices across Africa.

What ScreenIQ Does

Everything your screens need.
Nothing you don't.

Built for organisations that need reliable, centrally-managed digital content — without the complexity or cost of Western enterprise solutions.

Centralized Screen Management

Add, remove, and update any screen from one web dashboard. No need to visit each screen physically — one change updates all locations instantly.

Offline-First Playback

Content is cached locally on each device. When internet drops — and it will — your screens keep playing. No blank screens, no embarrassment.

Queue & Wayfinding Display

Show live queue numbers, department directions, and floor maps. Critical for hospitals, government offices, banks — anywhere people wait in queues.

Mobile Money Integration

Display live MTN MoMo and Airtel Money payment prompts, USSD codes, and QR codes automatically. Drive cashless adoption at the point of service.

Role-Based Content Control

Branch managers can update their local screens; head office controls the brand-wide content. Permissions are granular — the right people see the right controls.

Analytics & Uptime Reports

Know which screens are online, what content played, and for how long. Monthly uptime reports help you prove ROI to management.

How It Works

Up and running in
under 10 minutes.

No specialist engineers. No hardware rip-and-replace. If it has a screen and an HDMI port, ScreenIQ can run on it.

1

Plug in a low-cost media player

A Raspberry Pi, Amazon Fire Stick, or Android TV box. Any device with an HDMI port and browser works. We recommend hardware under $30.

2

Log into the ScreenIQ dashboard

Your account is cloud-based. Log in from any browser — laptop, phone, or tablet. Each screen gets a unique pairing code.

3

Upload or design your content

Use the drag-and-drop editor to build layouts, or upload images and videos directly. Schedule content per screen or per location.

4

Go live — and monitor remotely

Content pushes to screens in seconds. The dashboard shows you which screens are live, offline, or scheduled — from anywhere.
